# Strategic OKR Crafting Guidelines

## Core Philosophy

Singular is an **AI Transformation Partner**, not a dev shop. OKRs must reflect strategic outcomes, not feature delivery. When crafting OKRs for a kickoff presentation, think like a **consultant and strategist** advising the client's business, not a project manager tracking tasks.

## Rules

### What makes a GOOD OKR

- **Objectives** answer "why are we doing this?" — not "what are we building?"
- **Key Results** are measurable outcomes with specific metrics — percentages, rates, counts, scores, or zero-target thresholds
- OKRs focus on business impact: market validation, competitive positioning, investment readiness, risk reduction, scalability, revenue enablement
- OKRs should be achievable within the engagement phase (don't set KRs that depend on future phases)

### What makes a BAD OKR

- Feature-oriented: "Build authentication flow" or "Launch photo gallery"
- Vague: "Improve the product" or "Make it better"
- No metric: "Complete the audit" (vs "Complete 100% of the audit within Sprint 1")
- Depends on unplanned work: "Achieve 10K users" when no launch is planned in Phase 1

### OKR Structure

Always generate exactly **2 Objectives**, each with **3 Key Results**.

**Objective format**: A strategic goal statement that frames the business outcome.
Example: "Establish a validated, secure product foundation that unlocks confident investment in growth phases"

**Key Result format**: A measurable outcome with a specific metric.
Example: "Reduce critical and high-severity bugs in the core user journey to 0, achieving demo-ready state"

### Metric Types to Use

- **Completion percentages**: "Complete 100% of the technical audit..."
- **Zero-target thresholds**: "Reduce critical bugs to 0..."
- **Clarity/confidence scores**: "Deliver a Phase 2 investment case with ≥80% scope clarity..."
- **Velocity/capacity rates**: "Maintain sprint velocity at ≥90% of planned capacity..."
- **Usability scores**: "Validate experiences scoring ≥4/5 in internal usability review..."
- **Coverage metrics**: "Achieve 100% architectural scalability readiness..."
- **Time-bound delivery**: "...within Sprint 1 (Week 2)" or "...by end of Phase 1"

### Framing by Project Phase

**Stabilization/Foundation phases** (common for Phase 1):
- Objective themes: de-risking, validation, building confidence for investment, technical readiness
- KR themes: audit completion, bug reduction, demo-readiness, scope clarity for next phase

**Growth/Community phases** (common for Phase 2):
- Objective themes: user engagement, retention, network effects, product-market fit validation
- KR themes: retention rates, engagement metrics, feature adoption rates, NPS scores

**Monetization/Scale phases** (common for Phase 3):
- Objective themes: revenue enablement, market entry, competitive positioning, sustainable growth
- KR themes: conversion rates, revenue milestones, scalability metrics, market share indicators

### AI Transformation Lens

Since Singular positions as an AI Transformation Partner, at least one objective should consider how AI capabilities create differentiation or competitive advantage for the client. Think about:
- AI-enabled experiences that competitors don't offer
- Automation that reduces operational burden
- Data-driven decision making
- Time-to-market acceleration through AI tooling

## OKR Approval Process

1. Present both objectives with all 6 key results to the user
2. Explain the strategic reasoning behind each objective
3. Wait for explicit approval
4. If the user asks for changes, iterate — do not generate the presentation until OKRs are approved
