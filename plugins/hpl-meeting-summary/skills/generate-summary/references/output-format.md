# Meeting Summary Output Format

The goal is a **full strategic brief** — not a quick recap. Every summary should give someone who wasn't on the call a complete picture of who the client is, what they're dealing with, what was discussed, and exactly what needs to happen next. When in doubt, add more detail, not less.

---

## Full Format — Sales / Discovery / Client Call

This is the default format for any call with an external participant. Every section below is required. Do not shrink or skip sections.

```
📋 *Call Breakdown — [Client Name or Meeting Title]*

*Who They Are*
[2–4 sentences: who the client is, what their business does, their role, their technical sophistication level, and any relevant context about where they are in their journey (early-stage, scaling, already using AI, etc.). Be specific — avoid generic descriptions.]

*Context & Current State*
[2–4 sentences: what tools or systems they are currently using, what their core workflow looks like today, and what is NOT working. Reference specific tools, platforms, or processes they mentioned by name.]

*Pain Points*
[Number each pain point. For each one, include: the problem, a direct quote if they said something specific, and why it matters operationally. Minimum 3 pain points, no maximum.]

1. [Pain point title]
"[Direct quote if available]"
[1–2 sentences explaining the operational impact or urgency]

2. [Pain point title]
"[Direct quote if available]"
[1–2 sentences explaining the operational impact or urgency]

3. [Pain point title]
[Explanation if no direct quote]

*What Was Discussed*
[Cover each major section or chapter of the call. Each item should be a full sentence or two — not just a label. Capture what was actually said, decided, or explored.]

• [Topic]: [What was covered, what was decided or left open]
• [Topic]: [What was covered, what was decided or left open]
• [Topic]: [What was covered, what was decided or left open]

*Key Questions Raised*
[List any open questions, blockers, or unresolved items from the call. These are things that still need answers before moving forward.]

• [Question or blocker]
• [Question or blocker]

*Options / Directions Considered*
[If multiple paths were discussed (approaches, phases, scopes), list them clearly with a brief read on each.]

Option A — [Name]: [What it is, why it might work, any risk or downside]
Option B — [Name]: [What it is, why it might work, any risk or downside]
Option C — [Name]: [What it is, why it might work, any risk or downside]

[Omit this section if no options were discussed — but include it whenever there was any strategic decision or scoping conversation.]

*Strategic Read*
[1–3 sentences: your honest read on this client — their urgency, their fit, their sophistication, what will win them over, and what could lose them. This is the "bottom line" section. Make a clear recommendation if one exists.]

🔴 *Action Items*
• [Assignee first name]: [specific task — no vague items like "follow up"]
• [Assignee first name]: [specific task with deadline if mentioned]
• [Assignee first name]: [specific task]

🔴 *Next Steps / Timeline*
• [Specific date or event]: [what happens, what is due, what is expected]
• [Specific date or event]: [follow-up call, deliverable, or milestone]

🔗 Full report: [report_url]
```

---

## Critical Rules

- **Never write just one paragraph for a client call.** If the meeting has external participants, use the Full Format with every section filled out.
- **Use direct quotes** whenever the client said something specific, memorable, or revealing. Quotes make the summary feel real and trustworthy.
- **Name specific tools, systems, and products** — not generic references like "their current tool." Say Asana, GoHighLevel, Airtable, etc.
- **Pain points must be numbered** and each one must explain *why it matters*, not just what it is.
- **Strategic Read is mandatory for client calls** — it should feel like a clear internal handoff note, not a neutral summary.
- **Action items must be specific** — "Amilkar: send the scoping doc by Thursday 3PM CT" not "follow up with client"
- **Timelines must be specific** — "Thursday @ 3PM CT" not "soon" or "next week"
- Never use filler phrases: "the team discussed", "it was mentioned", "moving forward", "as discussed"
- Always write action items with the assignee's first name first
